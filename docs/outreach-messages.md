# Outreach Messages for Wireframe Feedback

## Message to Hannah Choi

Hi Hannah! Hope you're doing well.

I'm reaching out because we're working on something new at AhaSlides and I'd love your perspective on it.

Here's the short version: we've been hearing from trainers that when a live AhaSlides session ends, the learning stops. Trainers who want to offer self-paced learning end up stitching together 5+ tools (Canva, Loom, Genially, workbooks, hosting platforms) just to deliver one async lesson. We want to fix that.

We're building a Lesson Publisher — a way for trainers to create self-paced lessons directly inside AhaSlides. You'd be able to import an existing presentation or build from scratch, add blocks (text, video, quizzes, file attachments), hit publish, and get a shareable link. Learners go through it at their own pace, and you see their progress and quiz scores in a dashboard.

I've put together an early wireframe showing what this could look like. It has 4 screens: the lesson editor, a learner registration page, the learner view, and a trainer dashboard. Would you be open to taking a look and sharing your honest thoughts?

Given how creatively you use AhaSlides in your coaching sessions, I think your perspective would be really valuable — especially around what would make this work for learners who need flexibility and different pacing.

No rush at all. Let me know if you're interested and I'll send the wireframe over!

## Message to Mark Cunzolo

Hi Mark! Following up on the e-learning project thesis I shared with you earlier.

We've turned that into an interactive wireframe — 4 screens showing the Lesson Publisher in action:

1. **Lesson Editor** — block-based editor where you build lessons with text, video, quizzes, file attachments. Import from existing AhaSlides presentations.
2. **Learner Registration** — simple name + email gate before learners access a lesson (no passwords, no account creation).
3. **Learner View** — clean self-paced reading flow with progress tracking, inline quizzes with instant feedback, and a "Mark lesson complete" button.
4. **Trainer Dashboard** — two tabs: one showing all your lessons with stats, another showing individual learners with their progress, last active date, quiz scores, and completion status.

I know you've been vocal about needing better data tracking and centralized control for your instructors. The per-learner tracking in the dashboard was actually inspired by feedback from another champion user who said the same thing — trainers need to see specifically who is learning what, not just aggregate numbers.

Would love to get your take on this, especially:
- Does the dashboard give you what you'd need for reporting?
- How would this fit alongside your current AhaSlides workflow with your PRI instructors?
- Anything missing that would be a dealbreaker?

I'll send the wireframe file — you can open it in any browser and click through all 4 screens. Let me know when you have a few minutes to look!
