## Problem Statement

When a live AhaSlides session ends, the learning stops. Trainers who need ongoing, self-paced training are forced to stitch together 5+ separate tools (Canva for slides, Loom for video, Genially for gamification, workbooks, System.io for hosting). AhaSlides wants to add a course builder so trainers can deliver self-paced e-learning without leaving the platform.

AhaSlides already supports a "self-paced" mode but it's too limited for e-learning in any meaningful way.

## Demand Evidence

Three independent demand signals across different segments and geographies:

1. **Vani (KiotViet, Vietnam)** — Corporate L&D exec, former power user and customer champion. Churned after publishing her training materials as a self-paced online course built with Gemini Code. She told the team directly: she no longer had enough day-to-day experience with AhaSlides to answer questions about it. "We didn't lose Vani to a competitor. We lost her to a use case we don't support yet."

2. **Barbara Reynaud (Reunion Island, France)** — Independent trainer. Current workflow: Canva → Loom → Genially → downloadable workbook → System.io. Five tools duct-taped together. Her training is CPF-funded (French government training credits). She specifically asked AhaSlides to build this and said: "I thought it would be child's play for you."

3. **Daniel Nguyen (Vietnam)** — Train-the-trainers expert. Said: "Trainers don't need a full-fledged LMS that's hard to use. They just need to turn a lesson into a landing page." Wants to publish on his own website, with auto-graded assessments. Tried Thinkific and loved its simplicity. Flagged that designing self-paced content is harder than live because you lose real-time feedback.

Three segments (corporate L&D, independent trainer, train-the-trainers). Two geographies (Vietnam, France). All pointing at the same gap.

## Status Quo

**Barbara's 5-tool stack:** Canva for slides → Loom for video → Genially for gamification → downloadable workbook → System.io for hosting. Every async course requires stitching these together manually.

**Vani's escape:** Built her own e-learning site with Gemini Code rather than leave the use case unserved. The site lives at kiotvietlnd.github.io.

**Daniel's approach:** Uses Thinkific. Loves the simplicity and learner management. Explicitly said "one of the most important LMS features is managing learners and learning outcomes — Thinkific does this very well."

**The common pattern:** Trainers either duct-tape tools together, build their own, or leave for a dedicated course platform. AhaSlides loses them in all three cases.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**First customer:** Independent trainers like Barbara and Daniel. They decide alone (no procurement, no IT gatekeepers). Their current workaround is painful (5+ tools). They're already on AhaSlides.

**Narrowest wedge:** Lesson Publisher — trainers import an AhaSlides presentation or build from scratch, add async-native activities (quizzes, assessments, text, video, file attachments), hit publish, get a shareable URL. Learners complete it at their own pace. Trainer sees a simple results dashboard with completion rates and quiz scores.

This is Daniel's insight crystallized as a product: "turn a lesson into a landing page."

**What it's NOT (yet):** Not a full course hierarchy (Course → Sections → Lessons comes in v2). Not the live-to-async bridge. Not a full LMS. Not competing with Moodle or Blackboard.

## Constraints

- Must feel like AhaSlides — simple, clean, not cluttered. Trainers chose AhaSlides over alternatives because it was easy to use. The course builder must maintain that DNA.
- Independent trainers are the first segment. Corporate features (SSO, enterprise admin, SCORM) are later.
- Async activities are purpose-built for self-paced context, not mechanical conversions of live slide types. But trainers can import existing presentations and the system generates appropriate async activities from that content.
- AhaSlides already has a self-paced mode that's too limited. The new product extends beyond it rather than patching it.

## Premises

1. **Independent trainers (not corporate L&D) are the right first segment.** They decide alone, have the most painful workaround, and are already on AhaSlides.
2. **A lesson publisher + results dashboard is enough to replace the 5-tool stack.** Barbara doesn't need course hierarchy to switch — she needs a place to build and host lessons with interactive activities and see learner results.
3. **Trainers can import existing AhaSlides presentations.** Import brings over content and structure. Async activities are auto-generated from the presentation context. Purpose-built async activities can also be created from scratch. (Revised from original "fully separate" premise after cross-model challenge.)
4. **AhaSlides' existing user base is the distribution channel.** Upsell existing trainers into a higher-tier plan that includes lesson publishing.
5. **Clone Thinkific's UI simplicity with LearnWorlds' feature depth where it matters.** Thinkific is praised for clean, intuitive UX. LearnWorlds has serious-trainer features but cluttered UI. Take the best of both.
6. **The live-to-async bridge is the moat, but it's v2.** V1 proves the lesson publisher works. V2 adds seamless conversion between live sessions and self-paced lessons.

## Cross-Model Perspective

A Claude subagent provided an independent cold read:

**Steelman:** AhaSlides is the only engagement-first platform with hundreds of thousands of existing presentations and an active trainer base. By adding a lightweight course builder, they turn a retention problem (churned power users) into a new revenue line. In v2, the live-to-async bridge becomes a genuine moat no course platform can replicate.

**Key insight:** Daniel's quote ("just need to turn a lesson into a landing page") defines the ceiling of complexity. Build a lesson publisher first. Call it a course builder later.

**Challenged premise:** Premise 3 (async activities should be separate from live slides). Argument: all three demand signals are about reducing effort, not better async pedagogy. Telling a trainer with 50 presentations to rebuild from scratch saves them nothing. Proposed test: fake-door test with "Convert in one click" vs. "Build new async activities." If conversion wins 2:1, the bridge isn't v2 — it's v1.

**Resolution:** Revised to middle path. Import presentations, auto-generate appropriate async activities. Effort reduction without the design trap of running live polls in async mode.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Lesson Publisher (Chosen — Minimal Viable)

Trainers import a presentation or build from scratch, add ordered blocks (text, image, video, quiz, file), publish, get a shareable URL. Learners complete it. Trainer sees results.

- Effort: S-M (human: ~3-4 weeks / CC+gstack: ~2-3 days prototype)
- Risk: Low
- Ships fastest. Validates core value prop before building infrastructure.
- Cons: No course hierarchy. Might feel minimal for Thinkific/LearnWorlds refugees.

### Approach B: Course Builder (Ideal Architecture — Future)

Full Thinkific-style: Course → Sections → Lessons. Enrollment, learner accounts, progress tracking.

- Effort: M-L (human: ~8-12 weeks)
- Risk: Medium (scope creep)
- Feature-complete enough to replace System.io entirely.

### Approach C: AI Course Generator (Creative — Enhancement)

AI transforms a presentation into a complete self-paced course. Breaks into lessons, generates activities, writes assessments.

- Effort: M (human: ~6-8 weeks)
- Risk: Medium-High (AI quality, trainer trust)
- Massive differentiator. Solves Daniel's point that designing self-paced is hard.

**Sequence:** A → B → C layered on top.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach A: Lesson Publisher.** Ship in weeks, validate with Barbara and Daniel. Build toward B (Course Builder) once validated. Layer C (AI Generator) as an enhancement.

Three screens:
1. **Lesson Editor** — block-based (text, image, video, quiz, file attachment). Import from AhaSlides button. Publish & Get Link.
2. **Learner View** — clean reading flow, progress bar, inline quizzes with instant feedback, downloadable attachments.
3. **Trainer Dashboard** — lesson cards with learner count, completion %, avg quiz score.

## Open Questions

1. **Pricing model:** Per-learner seats? Higher-tier plan? Usage-based? This shapes the revenue model.
2. **Learner identity:** Do learners need accounts? Or can they complete lessons with just a name/email? Accounts add friction but enable progress tracking across lessons.
3. **Custom domains:** Daniel wants to publish on his own website. How important is this for v1?
4. **CPF compliance:** Barbara's training is CPF-funded. Are there regulatory requirements for the course platform (completion certificates, time tracking)?
5. **Existing self-paced mode:** How does the new Lesson Publisher relate to the current limited self-paced mode? Replacement? Extension? Parallel product?
6. **Mobile experience:** How many learners will access lessons on mobile? This affects the learner view design significantly.

## Success Criteria

1. Barbara sends a published lesson to learners and stops using at least 2 of her 5 current tools within 30 days.
2. Daniel publishes a lesson and gets learner completions within 2 weeks.
3. 10+ existing AhaSlides users publish lessons within the first month of beta.
4. Lesson completion rate > 50% (higher than industry average of 20-30% for pure self-paced).
5. Net retention improvement: measurable reduction in trainer churn for users who adopt Learning.

## Distribution Plan

This is a new product module within the existing AhaSlides platform — not a standalone product. Distribution is through the existing AhaSlides user base:

- Upsell flow within AhaSlides dashboard ("Turn your presentations into self-paced lessons")
- Published lessons hosted on AhaSlides domain (e.g., learn.ahaslides.com/{trainer}/{lesson-slug})
- Email campaign to existing trainers who have 5+ presentations
- In-app nudge when a trainer shares a presentation: "Want learners to complete this at their own pace?"

No separate CI/CD needed — ships as part of the AhaSlides platform.

## Dependencies

- Access to AhaSlides presentation data model for the import feature
- AhaSlides design system / component library for consistent UI
- Backend infrastructure for learner progress tracking (new data model)
- Decision on learner identity (accounts vs. anonymous)
- Alignment with AhaSlides product team on how this relates to the existing self-paced mode
