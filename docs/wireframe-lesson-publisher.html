Lesson 4: Obtain your official registration number (NDA)

By Barbara Reynaud • ~20 min • Self-paced
Before delivering your first training session in France, you must obtain your official registration number (NDA — Numéro de Déclaration d'Activité). This is not optional: it is a legal requirement for any individual or organization providing professional training.

The NDA is issued by the DREETS (Direction Régionale de l'Économie, de l'Emploi, du Travail et des Solidarités) in your region. Without it, you cannot legally invoice for training services or access public funding like CPF.
[ NDA Application Process — Step-by-step diagram ]
▶
Video: Filing with the DREETS — a walkthrough (6:45)
Required documents for your NDA application:

• A signed copy of your first training agreement or contract
• The Cerfa n°10782 form (declaration of activity)
• Proof of your legal status (Kbis extract or SIREN certificate)
• A bulletin n°3 from the criminal record (casier judiciaire)
• Your training program with objectives, duration, and methods

Tip: Prepare all documents before starting the online filing. Incomplete applications are the #1 cause of delays.

Knowledge Check (3 questions)

Question 1 of 3

Within how many days of your first training contract must you file for your NDA?

○ 15 days
○ 30 days
● 90 days
○ 6 months
✓ Correct! You have 90 days from signing your first training agreement to file your declaration with the DREETS.
Common mistakes that delay your NDA:

• Submitting an incomplete Cerfa form (missing signatures or dates)
• Not attaching a real training contract (a draft won't be accepted)
• Confusing the DREETS with other administrative bodies
• Waiting too long after your first contract — the 90-day window is strict

Once approved, your NDA is valid indefinitely, but you must file an annual activity report (BPF) to keep it active.
